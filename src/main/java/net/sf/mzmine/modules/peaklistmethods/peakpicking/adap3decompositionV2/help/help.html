Improved Spectral deconvolution

This method finds analytes and their fragmentation mass spectra by performing the following steps: by combining peaks with similar retention times into clusters and using a blind source separation method to determine model peaks and construct fragmentation mass spectra.

  1. Similar EIC peaks are combined into clusters. For each EIC peak, we define its retention time as the time corresponding to the peak apex and retention-time range as the interval corresponding to the peak intensity values above 5% of its apex intensity. Then the distance between two EIC peaks is equal to

    • zero if the retention-time range of one EIC peak is a subset of the retention-time range of the other,
    • infinity if the retention-time ranges of the two EIC peaks do not intersect,
    • absolute distance between retention times of the two EIC peaks otherwise.

    The EIC peaks are clustered by DBSCAN clustering algorithms and users need specify minimum distance between clusters and minimum cluster size. If preview is selected, the top right plot displays the result of the clustering with dots corresponding to EIC peaks and colors corresponding to different clusters.

  2. The number and shape of model peaks are determining by performing the non-negative matrix decomposition with the unimodality constrain. The number of model peaks are determined so that the shape of each model peak would be similar to the shape of at least one EIC peaks. The Peak-Similarity tolerance is used when the similarity is estimated.
